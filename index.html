Bio

A motif that has dominated much of my life is problem solving. I began contest math and competitive programming at ages 9 and 12, respectively. My competitive career eventually found moderate success with awards in various contests such as the CCO, USAMO, and APMO. Eventually, these hobbies that began as simply ways to supplement my learning has evolved into a lifelong interest in tackling the hardest problems around the world.

Throughout my formative years, I have continously sought out ways to challenge myself and spur my growth. These ventures include attending Toronto's premiere science and math program, TOPS; a research experience at Scholar Launch; and attending Canada's top-ranking engineering program at UWaterloo.

Interests

Graph Theory

Graph theory is an interesting intersection of computer science and combinatorics, the area of math I'm strongest in. Whether by exposure or innate affinity, I've found the solutions graph theory problems to be particularly elegant. Tarjan's Algorithm for finding strongly connected components has stuck with me particularly vividly.

Although I am not super invested in P vs. NP, I personally believe that the NP-complete version of the Travelling Salesman Problem does not have a polynomial-time solution, which would imply that P is not equal to NP. Though, of course, I cannot prove this.

Machine Learning

Machine learning has taken the world by storm, and shows seemingly unlimited potential in its ability to scour the internet for human knowledge and make accurate predictions in outwardly unpredictable systems. However, Cathy O'Neil's Weapons of Math Destruction—albeit lacking in convincing details, the book contains many noteworthy examples—and the more recent allegations against UnitedHealthcare on their misuse of machine learning algorithms have shown that much of the field is grossly under-regulated. Not only does the legislation need to catch up, more transparent machine learning models and concrete mathematical metrics must be developed in order to demand accountability.
